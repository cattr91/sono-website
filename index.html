Resilience is not a snapshot.

Sono works in two layers: before capital is committed, and once an asset is operating. Each layer adds a different kind of intelligence.

Site Intelligence

The same plot can look very different depending on what grid capacity is actually available, whether the water basin is under stress, or how many competing facilities are already queued for permits nearby. Sono combines those signals into a single score, calibrated against a growing reference population of European sites, so you can compare candidates on a common basis before committing capital.

No instrumentation required. Relevant for site selection, acquisition due diligence, and portfolio reviews.

Once an asset is operating, Sono tracks two things at once: how conditions are drifting from what the facility was designed for, and where day-to-day inefficiencies are costing money right now.

Continuous Monitoring

A facility built for 2018 climate assumptions is not the same facility in 2025. Free cooling hours shrink. Grid carbon intensity shifts. EnEfG deadlines approach. Sono tracks the gap between what an asset was designed for and what it is actually operating in, and translates that gap into a cost that appears on no balance sheet today.

Portfolio-level. No site visits. Relevant for asset managers, fund operators, and ESG teams who need to know where exposure is building before it becomes a write-down.

Operational Efficiency

Predictive maintenance, anomaly detection, and setpoint optimisation delivered to operations teams in plain language. Sono reads your existing historian, detects failure modes before alarms fire, traces root cause to specific equipment, and surfaces ranked recommendations with a confidence score.

No new hardware. No integration project. Historian-native. Physics-constrained so no physically impossible setpoint can ever be suggested.
